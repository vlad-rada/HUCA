BEHAVIOR

Their planet and its history

The planet where humans originate is named Earth. Data shows that Earth used to be a place with a varied climate, ranging from rainy forests with tall trees to dry and hot lands covered with sand, where few creatures could survive. At the present moment, the planet is in ruins. The lands are dry, with hard, cracked ground made of rock and dirt. The seas have risen from where they once were, leaving the continents small and old human structures submerged. Many humans live on elevated ground, far above sea level, in order to avoid their homes being flooded by the oceans.


Individual housing

Humans generally live in structures with their parents or with their mate and any children they may have. Some humans live alone, either by preference or because they do not have a mate, usually the latter. These structures generally include a private room for each human who resides in the household, mates sharing one room. These rooms store an area to sleep and any possessions kept by the human. There is another room for the purpose of eating, where food is stored in a cold box and cooked with a hot box. Some humans have a separate room for eating. Most humans also have a room for relaxation, where they can sit and use their entertainment devices, such as a "television," which is a box which gives off light that displays moving pictures.
